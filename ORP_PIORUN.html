History

ORP Piorun was an N-class destroyer operated by the Polish Navy in World War II. The word "Piorun" is Polish for "Thunderbolt." Ordered by the Royal Navy in 1939, the ship was laid down as HMS Nerissa before being loaned to the Poles in October 1940 while still under construction.

One of the most notable moments in ORP Piorun's service was during the hunt for the German battleship Bismarck in May 1941. Piorun located the Bismarck and bravely engaged it, drawing its fire and radioing its position to other Royal Navy forces. Though heavily outgunned, Piorun's actions helped ensure that other Allied ships could converge on Bismarck, ultimately leading to its sinking.

In addition to the Bismarck engagement, ORP Piorun was involved in various convoy protection duties across the North Atlantic. These convoys were vital to maintaining the flow of supplies and troops during the war. Piorun frequently faced threats from German U-boats and aircraft, showcasing the bravery and determination of her crew.

After the war, ORP Piorun was returned to the Royal Navy and recommissioned as HMS Noble. She continued to serve until being decommissioned and scrapped in 1955. Despite her relatively short service life, Piorun remains a symbol of the courage and resilience of the Polish Navy during World War II.

Today, the legacy of ORP Piorun is celebrated in Poland as a testament to the country's contributions to the Allied victory in World War II. Memorials and stories of her crew's heroism continue to inspire generations.
